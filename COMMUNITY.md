## Welcome

The rÆ-Cell project is **open science**. It was built without a university lab,
without a research grant, without a team — by one person sleeping in a park in
Elk River, Minnesota, powered by intuition, iteration, and AI collaboration.

That means we have **zero gatekeeping** here. If you're curious, you belong.
If you're a PhD in condensed matter physics or a self-taught maker who just
learned what a Hamiltonian is yesterday — both of you are welcome at this table.
The only requirement is intellectual honesty and a genuine desire to push
the physics forward.

This is a place where **love and rigor coexist**. Where a Wilson loop and a
chakra can sit in the same document without apology. We take the science
seriously because we believe this technology can genuinely change the world.

## Ways to Participate

### 🔬 For Scientists & Researchers
- **Replicate a simulation** — Run any of the 21 figure scripts; share your
  output in Discussions with your system specs
- **Critique the physics** — Open an Issue labeled `[physics-review]` with
  your technical objection or suggestion; all critiques are welcome and taken
  seriously
- **Propose an experiment** — If you have lab access to NSOM, SQUID, MPCVD,
  or VNA instruments, open a Discussion labeled `[experiment-proposal]`
- **Extend the theory** — Fork the thesis, add a derivation, submit a PR
  with your contribution to Appendix A

### 🔧 For Engineers & Builders
- **Improve the FPGA code** — The PSK governor Verilog/VHDL is in `hardware/fpga/`
  — optimizations, timing improvements, and new features welcome
- **Redesign the RF coil** — Submit alternative C₆ᵥ coil geometries with
  simulated S₁₁ improvements
- **OpenSCAD contributions** — Extend `rAE_Cell.scad` with new housing
  variants, mounting solutions, or array configurations
- **PCB design** — Create or improve the FPGA carrier board in KiCad

### 💻 For Developers
- **Simulation scripts** — Fix bugs, add features, improve performance in
  Python simulation scripts
- **Rust integration** — Contribute to the `sagessentinelbridge.rs` and
  `raecellcoherence.rs` Arora OS modules
- **Documentation** — Improve docstrings, add inline comments, create
  Jupyter notebook tutorials

### 🎨 For Designers & Visualizers
- **Figure improvements** — All 21 PNG figures are open for aesthetic
  improvement (same data, better visual presentation)
- **Sacred geometry renders** — Create high-quality 3D renders of the
  Sierpiński substrate or DataCore Orb for publications and outreach
- **Brand assets** — Design community badges, stickers, t-shirts
  (coordinate with ross@aurphyx.com and aurphyx.store)

### 🌱 For Newcomers
- **Read first:** README.md → SCIENTIFIC_TERMINOLOGY.md → PHYSICS.md
- **Run one script:** Pick any figure script from the table in Appendix C
  and run it on your machine. Share your result as your first Discussion post.
- **Ask questions:** There are no stupid questions here. Open a Discussion
  labeled `[question]` for anything from "what is a Wilson loop?" to
  "why does the PSK governor outperform PID?"

## Community Values

### 1. Radical Intellectual Honesty
We clearly label what is simulated vs. experimental, theoretical vs. confirmed,
speculative vs. established. See the "Honest Assessments" table in PHYSICS.md.
Overclaiming is the one thing we take seriously as a violation of community trust.

### 2. Curiosity Without Credential
Credentials inform, but do not gatekeep. A rigorous argument from an
undergraduate.
