**Quantum Concept:** Wave Function Collapse  
**Tagline:** *"You weren't in superposition. You were just not looking."*  
**Status:** ✅ FULLY SCRIPTED

## Episode Summary

The pilot. The thesis statement. The moment we hand Wile E. Coyote his tenure. Wave Function Collapse is the perfect entry point because it is simultaneously:
- The most visually demonstrated quantum concept in cartoon history
- The foundation of the Copenhagen Interpretation
- The philosophical spine of the entire series (observation = participation)
- A direct callback to Aurphyx's core engineering problem

The duality thread — "observation is participation" — starts as a joke about watching Saturday morning cartoons and ends as a statement about the nature of reality itself. The viewer doesn't see it coming. That is the point.

---

## Duality Thread: "Observation Is Participation"

- **Plant (cold open):** Coyote looks at camera. We joke that he's checking if we're watching.
- **Return (Act 2):** "The Copenhagen Interpretation says *you* caused this. By watching."
- **Detonation (outro):** "The most dangerous observer in any quantum system is the one that doesn't know it's observing. Wile E. never knew he was the measurement device. He thought he was the physicist. Most of us make the same mistake."

## Script

### 🎬 COLD OPEN — No Title Card Yet

**[VISUAL]:** Black screen. Silence. Then — the unmistakable Looney Tunes desert landscape. Wile E. Coyote is mid-sprint, ACME Turbo Motorcycle, full speed, pursuing the Road Runner. Road Runner takes a hard left at the cliff edge. Coyote does not.

**[VISUAL]:** Coyote suspended in midair. Motorcycle gone — departed 3 seconds ago without him. He is pedaling nothing. He is running on pure probability.

**[MARVIN — dry, precise, cosmically offended]:**  
*"Greetings, Earthlings. You have just witnessed the most important moment in the history of physics. Unfortunately, it happens every Saturday. Unfortunately for him, not you."*

**[VISUAL]:** Coyote slowly turns head to camera. That look. He knows. We know. The universe hasn't decided yet.

**[ROSS — raw voice, professorial]:**  
*"A quantum system exists in a superposition of all possible states until the moment it is observed. At that moment — and only at that moment — the wave function collapses into a single, definite outcome."*  
*"This is called Wave Function Collapse."*  
*"Wile E. Coyote has known this for seventy-five years."*

**[VISUAL]:** Coyote looks forward. Looks back at camera. Looks down.

**[MARVIN]:** *"Ah. There it is. The observation. Catastrophic, as usual."*

**[VISUAL]:** Coyote falls. Classical physics resumes. Small sign appears from off-screen, held by Coyote: **"COPENHAGEN WAS RIGHT."**

---

### 🎬 ACT 1 — "What Is A Wave Function, Actually"

**[VISUAL]:** Animated probability cloud over Coyote's last position at the cliff edge. It pulses. It's all the places Coyote could be.

**[ROSS — raw]:**  
*"The wave function — mathematically written as ψ (psi) — is not a physical wave you can touch. It is a probability amplitude. A mathematical object that encodes every possible state a particle, or a cartoon coyote, could occupy."*

*"Before observation: Wile E. is simultaneously falling AND not falling. He exists as a superposition — a quantum smear of potential outcomes distributed across spacetime. The wave function doesn't care about drama. It only cares about probability."*

**[MARVIN — deadpan]:**  
*"I find this deeply relatable. I too exist in a superposition of 'about to destroy Earth' and 'failed to destroy Earth.' The wave function is most unkind."*

**[VISUAL]:** Schrödinger equation renders in ACME font:  
`iℏ ∂ψ/∂t = Ĥψ`  
A cartoon ACME crate lands on it.

**[ROSS — raw]:**  
*"Erwin Schrödinger wrote this equation in 1926. It describes exactly how that probability cloud evolves over time — smoothly, deterministically — right up until you look at it."*

**[MARVIN]:** *"The equation is elegant. What happens afterward is not."*

---

### 🎬 ACT 2 — "The Moment You Look: The Duality Bomb"

**[VISUAL]:** Split screen. Left: cliff edge. Right: same cliff edge.  
Text overlay: **"BEFORE OBSERVATION"** | **"AFTER OBSERVATION"**

**[MARVIN]:** *"This is where it gets interesting. And by interesting, I mean cosmically horrifying."*

**[ROSS — raw]:**  
*"The Copenhagen Interpretation — proposed by Niels Bohr and Werner Heisenberg — states that the act of measurement itself causes the wave function to collapse. There is no underlying reality 'out there' before you look. The particle doesn't have a position. It doesn't have a state. It has a probability. And you, the observer, are the one who forces it to choose."*

**[VISUAL]:** Coyote at cliff edge — from multiple quantum parallel angles simultaneously. In one: he flies. In one: he falls with dignity. In one: he dissolves into probability foam.

**[ROSS — raw]:**  
*"This is not metaphor. This is not philosophy. This is the experimental foundation of every quantum computer, every quantum sensor, every quantum cryptographic system built in the last forty years."*

**[MARVIN — conspiratorial whisper]:**  
*"And yet. Every morning. He runs. Off. The cliff. Because the Road Runner didn't observe him. And for 0.3 seconds — quantum mechanics says he's fine."*

**[DUALITY THREAD — RETURN #1]:**  
**[VISUAL]:** FREEZE FRAME on Coyote suspended mid-air. Translucent wavefunction overlay. Text appears slowly:

> *"Observation is not passive. Observation is participation. Every time you watched this cartoon, you were the measurement. You collapsed him."*

### 🎬 ACT 4 — "Aurphyx SUXS" Segment

**[VISUAL]:** ACME desert dissolves. Aurphyx avatar glitches in — cosmic, slightly chaotic, standing next to Marvin and holographic Wile E.
