## Core Concept

The Heisenberg Uncertainty Principle: Δx · Δp ≥ ℏ/2 — you cannot simultaneously know a particle's exact position AND momentum. The more precisely you measure one, the less precisely you can know the other. Wile E. Coyote is the universe's best demonstration: when he's moving fast (high momentum certainty), he has no idea where he is (runs off cliff). When he stops to figure out where he is, he's already too late.
