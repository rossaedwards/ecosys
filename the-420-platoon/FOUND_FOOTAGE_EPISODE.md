# 📼 The 420 Platoon — The Found Footage Episode
## "TAPE_047.VHS" (Working Title)

> *One episode per season. The rules break. The show knows.*

---

## The Concept

Once per season, an episode is presented as **recovered VHS footage** — as if someone found a tape of the 420 Platoon on an unmarked cassette in a decommissioned bunker. The show's normal grammar is GONE:

- No Pop-Up Video bubbles
- No Corporal Frequencies
- No vinyl deck transitions
- No Dr. Satan commentary
- No commercial break claymation fights
- No Drill Sergeant

Just raw, degraded, VHS-quality footage of the Platoon in a situation that feels **genuinely wrong.**

---

## Visual Grammar

| Normal Episode | Found Footage Episode |
|---|---|
| Hyper-realistic 3D avatars | Same avatars, VHS filter, tracking lines |
| Pop-Up bubbles | Glitch artifacts where bubbles WOULD be |
| Clean transitions | Hard cuts, static, timestamp jumps |
| Dr. Satan breaks | Black screen with static, no audio |
| Drill Sergeant appears | Brief static flash where he WOULD appear |
| Vinyl needle drop | Record scratch sound — then silence |
| Corporal Frequencies | Empty corner where the deck usually sits |

The ABSENCE of the show's normal elements IS the horror.

---

## The Emotional Register

This episode plays **straight.** No jokes that land cleanly. The humor is uncomfortable. The anti-war thesis isn't delivered as a punchline — it's delivered as **dread.** This is the MASH Season 11 finale energy. The one that hurt.

The audience has been laughing for 7 episodes. Episode 8 makes them feel what was underneath the laughter the whole time.

## The Season 1 Version — "TAPE_047.VHS"

### COLD OPEN
- Static. Timestamp: 2045-03-17 04:23:11
- Camera found on the ground. Pointed at the sky.
- Sound of the Platoon nearby. Talking normally. Nobody holding the camera.
- Who left it running?

### ACT 1
- Normal mission. But something is off.
- The banter rhythm is slightly wrong — pauses too long, overlaps missing
- Chunky keeps looking at the camera. Nobody else does.
- Blaze: *"You feel that?"* Roxy: *"...Yeah."* Neither elaborates.
- Timestamp skip: 04:23:11 → 06:47:33. Two hours missing.

### WHERE THE BREAK WOULD BE
- Black screen. Static. 10 seconds.
- A single frame: claymation hand reaching toward camera.
- Gone. Back to footage.
- Dr. Satan never appears.

### ACT 2
- The Platoon has won. They're sitting in the aftermath.
- Nobody is celebrating.
- The Wall of Fallen is visible. More faces than should be there yet.
- Roxy looks at the wall. Looks at camera. Looks away.
- Vinyl scratch sound from nowhere. Silence.
- Corporal Frequencies' corner: empty. But the deck is on. Spinning. No needle.

### WHERE BREAK 2 WOULD BE
- Static. Then: Ash Williams, claymation, alone on screen.
- Not the post-credits version. This one looks different.
- He doesn't say "Groovy."
- He looks at camera for 4 seconds.
- He nods slowly.
- Static.

### ACT 3
- The Platoon packs up. Moving out.
- Blaze picks up the camera.
- Looks into it directly.
- *"Same time next week?"* — his usual closer.
- Beat.
- He doesn't look sure.
- Sets the camera back down.
- Platoon walks away.
- Camera runs. Empty scene. Wind.
- Timestamp continues for 47 seconds.
- Then: tape runs out.

### POST-CREDITS
- Not Ash saying "Groovy."
- Black screen.
- The Pop-Up bubble sound: WHOOP WHOOP
- A bubble appears. Empty. No text.
- Fades out.

---

## Why This Works

1. **The absence of the show's grammar IS the message** — strip the comedy away and you have the war
2. **Earned emotional gut punch** — 7 episodes of laughter makes Episode 8 hit harder
3. **Rewatch value explodes** — every prior episode gets recontextualized
4. **The empty Pop-Up bubble** is the thesis: the joke ran out. The war didn't.
5. **Ash's silent nod** — he's seen things. He's not joking this time. Even he knows.

---

## Season Rules

- Season 1: Episode 8 (finale)
- Season 2: Episode 5 (mid-season gut punch — they'll never expect it mid-run)
- Season 3: Episode 1 (cold open. No explanation. The tape is back. Different timestamp.)
