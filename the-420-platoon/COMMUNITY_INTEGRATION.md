# 🌍 The 420 Platoon — Community Integration Mechanic

> *"You're not watching the show. You're IN the show. Allegedly."

---

## THE FACE SUBMISSION SYSTEM

### The Mechanic

**Submit your 15 seconds. Get spliced into the episode.**

Every episode, a curated selection of fan-submitted faces are integrated as:
- Enemy soldiers (background to hero, depending on submission quality)
- Crowd avatars
- Background characters in the corporate dystopia
- Claymation fight audience members
- Historical crowd extras in Break 1 + 2

---

## Submission Pipeline

```
FAN SUBMITS:
  → 15-second face video (neutral expression, left, right, up, down)
  → Signed digital release form (Aurphyx LLC IP assignment)
  → Optional: 15-second audio submission ("your best war cry / roast / one-liner")

PRODUCTION RECEIVES:
  → Face scan processed into avatar system
  → Audio reviewed for episode-appropriate clips
  → Selected submissions notified pre-episode

IN THE EPISODE:
  → Face appears on background soldier, crowd member, or enemy
  → Pop-Up Video bubble: *"That's [username]. They submitted. Allegedly on purpose."
  → Audio clips used as background radio chatter, crowd noise, or combat audio
```

---

## The Engagement Tiers

| Tier | What They Get | How They Earn It |
|---|---|---|
| **Background Extra** | Face in crowd / enemy background | Any submission |
| **Named Extra** | Face + username in Pop-Up bubble | Selected by production |
| **Combat Audio** | Voice clip in episode | Outstanding audio submission |
| **Break Character** | Face in claymation audience (Breaks 1+2) | Fan favorite vote |
| **The Platoon Bench** | Recurring background platoon member | Season-long community vote |
| **Dr. Satan's Audience** | Claymation face in the Break 1 crowd | Wildcard — random draw |

## The "Most Wanted" Board

Every episode opens with a **"Most Wanted" board** in the bunker background — a cork board with photos of that episode's submitted faces listed as enemy combatants, with absurd fake crimes:

- *[username] — Wanted for: Crimes Against Vibes*
- *[username] — Wanted for: Aggressive Unbothered Energy*
- *[username] — Wanted for: Dropping a Joint in a Combat Zone*

This is visible in every bunker scene. Eagle-eyed fans pause and screenshot. **This IS the engagement mechanic.**

---

## The Audio Layer

Fan audio submissions become:
- Background radio chatter during combat
- Crowd noise in Break 1 fights
- "Intercepted enemy communications" (played as joke intel)
- The actual *"WHOOOP WHOOOP"* Pop-Up sound (fan-submitted version)
- Season finale: **full fan audio remix** as the vinyl deck closer

---

## Why This Is Genius

1. **Zero production cost for crowd scenes** — fans ARE the crowd
2. **Built-in social sharing** — everyone screenshots their own face
3. **Parasocial engagement without parasocial damage** — they're IN the satire, not above it
4. **The show satirizes war** — fans volunteering to be the enemy is *the thesis made interactive*
5. **Netflix / streaming algorithm loves it** — watch parties, pause-and-find culture, rewatch value explodes
6. **Legal simplicity** — digital release form, IP assignment, done
7. **Community self-selects** — the people who submit get the joke. The show finds its tribe.
