# 🏒 The 420 Platoon — The Letterkenny Dialogue Engine

> *"Give your balls a tug. You're gonna get yourself shot."*

---

## The Core Mechanic

The 420 Platoon's combat banter operates on **Letterkenny rhythm**:
- Rapid-fire overlapping volleys
- Increasingly absurd escalation
- Complete linguistic precision about nothing important
- The actual violence is the B-plot
- The wordplay is the A-plot

**The firefight happens in the background. The roast is the foreground.**

---

## The Combat Banter Structure

```
BEAT 1: Roxy opens with an observation about the enemy
BEAT 2: Blaze extends it with a metaphor
BEAT 3: Doc Lazlo adds a completely unrelated wine tasting note
BEAT 4: Chunky misunderstands and accidentally makes it worse
BEAT 5: All four deliver the punchline in overlapping rhythm
BEAT 6: [enemy does something catastrophically stupid in background]
BEAT 7: Four-beat pause. Everyone looks.
BEAT 8: Back to banter. Never acknowledged.
```

## The "Allegedly" Rule

Every episode contains at least one moment where a character describes something catastrophic in the most understated possible terms, ending with:
- *"...allegedly."
- *"...or so I've heard."
- *"...reportedly."

This is Letterkenny's legal disclaimer energy applied to war crimes. It works.
