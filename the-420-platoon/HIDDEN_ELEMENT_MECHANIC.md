# 🔍 The 420 Platoon — The Hidden Element Mechanic
## (Not Waldo. Ours.)

> *"Where's the Deck?"*

---

## The Concept

Every episode hides **Corporal Frequencies' vinyl deck** somewhere impossible in the background of a scene where it has no business being.

- In a crowd of claymation historical figures
- On a shelf in the enemy faction's headquarters
- Floating in the background of a battle scene, 2 pixels wide
- Visible through a window in a scene set in 2045 that looks out onto 1943 footage
- On the deck of a ship in an archival clip during a vinyl transition

**The deck is always there. Always wrong. Always findable.**

---

## The Engagement Loop

```
Episode drops
  → Community immediately starts hunting
  → First find gets posted to r/420Platoon
  → Screenshot goes viral within the community
  → Production never confirms or denies
  → Until the Season Finale
  → Where Corporal Frequencies addresses it directly
  → For exactly one line
  → Then never again
```

---

## The Escalation Rule

| Season | Difficulty | Special Rule |
|---|---|---|
| Season 1 | Moderate | One deck per episode |
| Season 2 | Hard | Sometimes TWO decks. One is fake. |
| Season 3 | Nightmare | The deck appears in the THEME SONG. Frame 7. |
| Season 4 | Cosmic | The deck is in the Notion page. No we're not joking. |

---

## Secondary Hidden Elements

Beyond the deck, each episode also hides:
- **One fan-submitted face** in a location that makes NO sense (inside a claymation painting, on a wanted poster in 1940s archival footage)
- **One Bruce Campbell reference** that isn't Ash — a prop, a poster, a book title in the background
- **One South Park / Letterkenny / MASH reference** that requires genuine knowledge to catch

---

## The Naming

The community calls it **"Deck Hunting."**
The show never calls it anything.
Production account on socials posts one cryptic emoji per episode the day before release.
That's it. That's the entire marketing strategy for this mechanic.
