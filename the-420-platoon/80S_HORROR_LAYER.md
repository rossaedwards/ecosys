# 🎃 The 420 Platoon — The 80s Horror Analogy Layer

> *"Every war movie is a horror movie with better PR."*

---

## The Concept

The 420 Platoon treats military dystopia through the lens of 80s horror because **they are the same genre**. Corporate fascism = Hellraiser Cenobites. Surveillance state = Videodrome. Biological warfare = The Fly. The horror analogies aren't jokes — they're accurate.

---

## The "They Live" Pop-Up Variant

Occasional Pop-Up bubbles render as **They Live sunglasses overlay** — character puts on sunglasses, hidden text reveals beneath corporate/military propaganda visible in the scene.

**Example:**
- Enemy faction banner reads: "FREEDOM THROUGH SECURITY"
- They Live overlay: "OBEY"
- Pop-Up bubble: *"Shepard Fairey called. He said 'too on the nose.' We said 'noted.' We kept it."

---

## VHS Aesthetic Layer

Specific episode segments get **VHS horror filter treatment**:
- Tracking lines
- Color bleed
- Static cuts
- Fake "PLAY" and "REC" indicators
- Used for: The Drop sequences, Dr. Satan segments, Corporal Frequencies transitions

This makes the most intense/absurd moments feel like **found footage** — reinforcing that this is all archival, all remix, all remembered.
