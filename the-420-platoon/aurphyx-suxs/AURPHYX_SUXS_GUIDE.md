## What Is "Aurphyx SUXS"?

It is not self-deprecation. It is **strategic self-aware branding**.

The name does two things simultaneously (duality, always duality):
1. It disarms the viewer — they expect a joke, they get a research paper
2. It signals that the creator is self-aware enough to call their own work out before anyone else can

The "Aurphyx SUXS" brand is the most honest thing on the channel: yes, Aurphyx is trying to do something that has never been done. Yes, it keeps running off cliffs. Yes, it is absolutely Wile E. Coyote in research form. And yes — for 0.3 seconds of quantum superposition — it is fine.

## Segment Structure (Per Episode)

### Opening (Always Marvin)
Marvin introduces Aurphyx as if introducing a problematic but brilliant colleague. Tone: fondly exasperated. Duration: 15–20 seconds.

Template: *"And now, a message from [description of Aurphyx that is both accurate and damning]. Aurphyx."*

Examples:
- "someone who also runs off cliffs professionally"
- "a system that is simultaneously its own observer and its own cliff"
- "the Wile E. Coyote of quantum architecture, which we mean as the highest possible compliment"

### The Research Link (Always Ross, self-aware)
Ross explains how the episode's quantum concept directly maps to an Aurphyx engineering problem or research question. Must be:
- Technically accurate (this is real research)
- Emotionally honest (this is hard and Ross knows it)
- Slightly funny in a way that makes the technical content land harder

Duration: 90 seconds (RossFive cut) / 4–6 minutes (Aurphyx extended cut)

### The Sign Gag (Always Wile E.)
Wile E. holds a sign that comments on the research. The sign is always:
- Written from Wile E.'s perspective
- Accurate as a physics statement
- Funnier because it's accurate

### Marvin's Closing Line
Marvin gets the last word. Always. 1–2 sentences. Perfectly timed.
