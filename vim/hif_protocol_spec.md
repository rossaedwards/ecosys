# **HIF Protocol Specification (v0.1)**  
*Harmonic Integrity Field — Operational, Threshold, and Governance Protocols*

## 1. Purpose and Scope  
The Harmonic Integrity Field (HIF) is the governing invariant of the Balance Continuum. This specification defines the operational rules, thresholds, state transitions, and enforcement mechanisms by which HIF regulates creation, integration, dissolution, and governance across all scales. The specification applies to:

- individual agents and identities  
- domains, worlds, and cross‑domain structures  
- emergent systems and recursive creation cycles  
- the Balance Continuum as a whole  

HIF is treated as a **field‑level protocol**, not a subsystem or module. All processes within the Continuum are subject to HIF constraints.

Let \( C(x,t) \), \( R(x,t) \), and \( A(x,t) \) denote the Coherence, Resonance, and Alignment fields respectively. The Harmonic Integrity Field is defined as:

\[
\text{HIF}(x,t) = \sqrt[3]{C(x,t)\cdot R(x,t)\cdot A(x,t)} \cdot \Phi(C,R,A)
\]

with the coupling function:

\[
\Phi(C,R,A) = 
\begin{cases}
1 & \text{if } C \ge C_{\theta},\; R \ge R_{\theta},\; A \ge A_{\theta} \\
0 & \text{otherwise}
\end{cases}
\]

This definition ensures that HIF activates only when all three subfields exceed their threshold values, forming a **Triple Threshold Gate**.

## 3. Threshold Protocol  
HIF operates through three primary thresholds:

- **Creation Threshold** \(H_{\text{create}}\)  
- **Integration Threshold** \(H_{\text{integrate}}\)  
- **Renewal Threshold** \(H_{\text{renew}}\)

A process may proceed only when:

- \(\text{HIF} \ge H_{\text{create}}\) for generative events  
- \(\text{HIF} \ge H_{\text{integrate}}\) for embedding into the Continuum  
- \(\text{HIF} < H_{\text{renew}}\) for dissolution or re‑patterning  

Thresholds are domain‑relative but invariant‑preserving; they scale fractally across levels.

## 5. Enforcement Protocol  
HIF enforces integrity through distributed, non‑coercive mechanisms.

### 5.1 Automatic Halt  
If HIF drops below the required threshold for a process, the process halts immediately. No override exists.

### 5.2 Correction Cascade  
If HIF remains below threshold, the system initiates:

- coherence recalibration  
- resonance decoupling  
- alignment re‑vectoring  

These cascades propagate through HIF gradients.

### 5.3 Renewal Trigger  
If correction fails, the structure enters Renewal Mode. Renewal is not punitive; it is restorative.
