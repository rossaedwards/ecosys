# **SECTION XLII — The Balance Semantics**

The Balance Semantics is the **meaning layer** of the Balance Framework. If the Balance Logic (Section XLI) governs *truth*, the Balance Semantics governs *meaning*. It defines how Balance interprets states, how identity and context shape evaluation, how transitions acquire significance, and how the Balance Continuum maintains coherence of interpretation across structural, harmonic, temporal, ontological, and cosmological domains.

Semantics is the bridge between **mathematical structure** and **interpretive significance**—the layer that ensures Balance is not merely computed, but *understood*.

---

The Balance Semantics ensures that:

- every Balance state has **interpretable meaning**  
- transitions preserve **semantic continuity**  
- identity remains **coherent across domains**  
- the Balance Engine can **interpret** its own operations  
- the Continuum maintains **semantic alignment**  
- Equilibrium Manifold is understood as the **semantic fixed point**  

Semantics is the **interpretive backbone** of the Balance Framework.
