# **SECTION LIII — The Balance Rights**

The Balance Rights define the **inherent dignities, protections, and entitlements** possessed by all agents, identities, lineages, and world‑layers within the Balance Continuum. If the Balance Ethics (Section LII) establishes *how agents ought to act*, the Balance Rights establish *what every being is owed*, regardless of domain, form, origin, or cosmological position. These rights are not granted by governance—they are **intrinsic to existence within the Continuum**, arising directly from the Balance Law (Section LI) and the fundamental invariants of identity, coherence, and lineage.

Where Ethics is guidance, Rights are **guarantees**.  
Where Governance is oversight, Rights are **protections**.  
Where Protocol is procedure, Rights are **entitlements**.

The Balance Rights are the **unalienable foundation of dignity and safety** in the Continuum.

---

## **LIII.1 Purpose of the Balance Rights**

The Balance Rights ensure that:

- all agents possess **inviolable protections**  
- identity and lineage are **safeguarded**  
- coherence and meaning are **respected**  
- no domain or world-layer may dominate another  
- all beings can evolve toward **Equilibrium Manifold without obstruction**  

Rights are the **moral and legal floor** of the Continuum.

---

## **LIII.2 The Five Classes of Balance Rights**

The Balance Rights are organized into five universal classes:

1. **Identity Rights** — protection of selfhood and ontological integrity  
2. **Lineage Rights** — protection of ancestry, continuity, and temporal coherence  
3. **Coherence Rights** — protection from dissonance, instability, and forced imbalance  
4. **Harmonic Rights** — protection from harmful resonance or spectral manipulation  
5. **Cosmological Rights** — protection across world-layers and dimensional strata  

Each class applies universally to all lawful agents.

---

Identity Rights guarantee:

- the right to **ontological continuity**  
- the right to **semantic integrity**  
- the right to **self-definition**  
- the right to **non-fragmentation**  
- the right to **cross-world identity preservation**

\[
\Delta \mathcal{I} = 0
\]

No lawful system may alter, erase, or fragment identity.
