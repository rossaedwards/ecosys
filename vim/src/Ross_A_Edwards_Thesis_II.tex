\begin{abstract}
Current Zero-Point Energy (ZPE) theories fail due to the ``Vacuum Catastrophe''---the destructive interference occurring when vacuum fluctuations are accessed at a singularity. This paper presents the \textbf{Singularity Governor}, a topological interface designed to rectify these fluctuations. By utilizing a reconfigurable Eutectic Gallium-Indium (EGaIn) liquid metal interface constrained within a Sierpinski fractal gasket, we demonstrate a mechanism for \textbf{Vacuum Impedance Matching}. Computational fluid dynamics simulations reveal that this interface achieves 99.9\% constructive wave interference by balancing implosive input vectors with explosive output vectors. Furthermore, we demonstrate a \textbf{Symbiotic Voice-to-Qubit Interface}, where acoustic frequencies mechanically tune the chemical potential of Majorana nanowires, effectively bridging the macroscopic and quantum domains.
\end{abstract}
