Dear Editor,

I am pleased to submit the enclosed manuscript, "Photonic Band Engineering in Hexagonal Resonant Lattices for Quantum Coherence Enhancement," for consideration as a Research Article in *Optica*.

This work presents a hexagonal photonic crystal lattice with C₆ᵥ symmetry that combines three decoherence suppression mechanisms—photonic band gaps, flatband localization, and fractal-sublattice-enhanced Anderson localization—within a single, fabrication-compatible platform. The principal quantitative result is a decoherence suppression ratio γ_fractal/γ_Euclidean = 0.63, corresponding to a 1.6× coherence time improvement, validated through plane-wave expansion calculations, tight-binding models, and Lindblad master equation simulations.

**Why this manuscript is appropriate for *Optica*:**

The work addresses a timely challenge at the intersection of photonic crystal engineering and quantum information science. While individual mechanisms (band gaps, flatbands, Anderson localization) have been demonstrated separately, their combination in a single lattice geometry is, to our knowledge, novel. The proposed femtosecond laser direct-write fabrication protocol in fused silica builds directly on experimental techniques pioneered in the photonics community (Schwartz *et al.*, Nature 2007; Rechtsman *et al.*, Nature 2013; Mukherjee *et al.*, PRL 2015), ensuring that the theoretical predictions presented here are experimentally testable with existing infrastructure.

**Key results include:**

- A complete photonic band gap with gap-midgap ratio of 21.4% in a 19-circle hexagonal configuration
- Flatband regions with group velocity v_g < 0.01c and Purcell factors F_P ~ 10³–10⁴
- Anderson localization on fractal sublattices (D_f = 1.585) demonstrating strong localization even at weak disorder (W/t₁ = 0.3)
- A concrete fabrication protocol with itemized budget ($105K) and 18-month timeline
- Quantitative comparison with existing platforms (silicon PhC slabs, SiN rings, Lieb/kagome lattices)

The manuscript contains 7 sections across approximately 10 pages, with 6 figures and 50 references. All simulation code will be made publicly available upon acceptance.

This manuscript has not been submitted elsewhere, and all results are original. I confirm that there are no conflicts of interest beyond those disclosed in the manuscript (RAE is the founder of Aurphyx LLC).
