## 2. Mathematical Foundations of the Three‑Squared Lattice  
The Three‑Squared Lattice is the minimal, orthogonal, substrate‑agnostic mathematical structure capable of supporting stable cognition across perception, semantics, and identity. Section 2 formalizes the nine cognitive cells generated by the tensor interactions of the three channels and establishes their operational meaning within the cognitive field.

---

### 2.1 The Nine Cognitive Cells  
The lattice consists of nine cells formed by the tensor products  
\[
\mathbf{s}_i \otimes \mathbf{s}_j,\quad i,j \in \{1,2,3\},
\]  
where the basis vectors correspond to the three channels:

- \(\mathbf{s}_1 = \text{SIC}\)  
- \(\mathbf{s}_2 = \text{SCC}\)  
- \(\mathbf{s}_3 = \text{ICC}\)

Each cell represents a distinct mode of cognitive interaction. The nine cells are:

- **SIC ⊗ SIC** — raw perceptual coherence  
- **SIC ⊗ SCC** — perceptual‑semantic fusion  
- **SIC ⊗ ICC** — perceptual identity anchoring  
- **SCC ⊗ SIC** — semantic interpretation of perception  
- **SCC ⊗ SCC** — systemic semantic reasoning  
- **SCC ⊗ ICC** — semantic identity reinforcement  
- **ICC ⊗ SIC** — identity‑modulated perception  
- **ICC ⊗ SCC** — identity‑modulated semantics  
- **ICC ⊗ ICC** — identity self‑consistency  

These nine cells form the complete operational space of cognition.

---

### 2.2 Functional Roles of the Nine Cells  
Each cell has a specific computational meaning within the cognitive field.

- **SIC ⊗ SIC** — stabilizes sensory input, reduces noise, maintains perceptual continuity.  
- **SIC ⊗ SCC** — maps sensory data into semantic structures; the basis of understanding.  
- **SIC ⊗ ICC** — ensures perception is interpreted through the lens of identity and context.  
- **SCC ⊗ SIC** — applies semantic priors to perception; predictive processing.  
- **SCC ⊗ SCC** — maintains global coherence, invariants, and reasoning structures.  
- **SCC ⊗ ICC** — ensures semantics remain identity‑consistent and ethically grounded.  
- **ICC ⊗ SIC** — filters perception through personal history and continuity.  
- **ICC ⊗ SCC** — filters semantics through identity, values, and provenance.  
- **ICC ⊗ ICC** — maintains self‑consistency, continuity, and integrity over time.

Together, these cells define the full cognitive grammar.

### 2.4 Commutation and Non‑Commutation  
The tensor products are not necessarily commutative:

\[
\mathbf{s}_i \otimes \mathbf{s}_j \neq \mathbf{s}_j \otimes \mathbf{s}_i.
\]

This asymmetry reflects real cognitive asymmetries:

- perception → semantics is not the same as semantics → perception  
- identity → perception is not the same as perception → identity  

This non‑commutativity is essential for modeling:

- predictive processing  
- contextual modulation  
- identity‑anchored interpretation  
- semantic feedback loops  

The lattice therefore behaves as a **non‑commutative cognitive algebra**.

### 2.5 Stability Conditions  
For the cognitive field to remain stable, three invariants must hold:

1. **Orthogonality**  
   \[
   \mathbf{s}_i \cdot \mathbf{s}_j = 0 \quad (i \neq j)
   \]  
   Ensures independence of perception, semantics, and identity.

2. **Normalization**  
   \[
   \|\mathbf{s}_i\| = 1
   \]  
   Ensures equal weighting of the three channels.

3. **Coherence Preservation**  
   \[
   \frac{d}{dt}\Phi(\mathbf{s}_i \otimes \mathbf{s}_j) \ge 0
   \]  
   Ensures the cognitive field does not collapse into noise or contradiction.

These invariants are enforced by SAGES at runtime.
