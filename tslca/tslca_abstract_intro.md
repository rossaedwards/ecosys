The Three‑Squared‑Lattice Cognitive Architecture provides a unified theoretical framework for understanding and engineering cognition at scales ranging from embodied agents to civilization‑level systems. Modern computational architectures typically isolate perception, reasoning, and identity into separate subsystems, resulting in fragmentation, incoherence, and brittle behavior under real‑world complexity. This whitepaper introduces a lattice‑based model that resolves these limitations by treating cognition as a structured field generated by three orthogonal channels whose interactions produce a coherent, self‑maintaining cognitive whole.

The Systemic Coherence Channel (SCC) governs semantics, invariants, and system‑level reasoning. The Identity‑Coherence Channel (ICC) governs continuity of self, provenance, and ethical constraints. Each channel is independently defined yet mutually interdependent, forming a triad that reflects the minimal structure required for stable, adaptive cognition.
