## 3. The Three Channels

The Three‑Squared‑Lattice Cognitive Architecture is built on three orthogonal cognitive channels. Each channel governs a distinct dimension of intelligence, and together they form the basis vectors of the cognitive lattice. This section defines each channel formally, establishes its operational domain, and clarifies its role within the broader cognitive field.

---

### 3.1 Symbiotic Integration Channel (SIC)

SIC governs the system’s coupling with the world. It is responsible for perception, accessibility, embodiment, and environmental attunement. In the lattice, SIC defines the **perceptual axis**, ensuring that cognition is grounded in real sensory data and contextual signals.

SIC includes:
- multimodal perception (visual, auditory, tactile, proprioceptive)
- accessibility transformations (SAIL, VAP, environmental overlays)
- sensorimotor integration and feedback loops
- embodied context (location, motion, orientation)
- environmental salience and affordance detection

SIC ensures that the cognitive field remains anchored to the external world and that all semantic and identity operations remain grounded in lived context.

---

### 3.2 Systemic Coherence Channel (SCC)

SCC governs semantics, reasoning, invariants, and system‑level coherence. It defines the **semantic axis** of the lattice, providing the structures that allow information to be interpreted, transformed, and maintained consistently across time.

SCC includes:
- semantic parsing and transmutation (Fuxyez, FUTE)
- invariant enforcement and coherence maintenance
- systemic reasoning and global context propagation
- rule‑based and field‑based interpretation
- integration with AuraFS as the topological semantic substrate

SCC ensures that cognition remains logically consistent, semantically stable, and capable of supporting complex reasoning across distributed or embodied systems.

---

### 3.3 Identity‑Coherence Channel (ICC)

ICC governs identity continuity, provenance, self‑consistency, and ethical grounding. It defines the **identity axis** of the lattice, ensuring that the system maintains a coherent sense of self across time, transformations, and contexts.

ICC includes:
- identity anchoring (SoulShot, BlissID)
- provenance tracking and GuardHash lattice binding
- ethical invariants and SAGES enforcement
- memory continuity and self‑referential stability
- value alignment and contextual self‑modulation

ICC ensures that cognition remains stable across time, that actions remain consistent with identity and values, and that all transformations preserve continuity of self.

---

### 3.4 Orthogonality of the Channels

The three channels are orthogonal in the cognitive vector space:
\[
\mathbf{s}_i \cdot \mathbf{s}_j = 0 \quad (i \neq j).
\]

This orthogonality ensures:
- perception does not collapse into semantics  
- semantics does not overwrite identity  
- identity does not distort perception  

Each channel is independent yet combinable, forming the basis for the nine‑cell lattice.

---

### 3.5 Interdependence and Mutual Reinforcement

Although orthogonal, the channels are interdependent. Cognition emerges from their interactions:
- SIC grounds SCC in reality.  
- SCC interprets SIC into meaning.  
- ICC stabilizes both through continuity and ethics.  

This triadic structure is the minimal configuration capable of supporting stable, reversible, identity‑preserving cognition.

---

Each channel generates a cognitive sub‑field:
\[

\]

The full cognitive field is the superposition:
\[
\Phi = \Phi_{\text{SIC}} + \Phi_{\text{SCC}} + \Phi_{\text{ICC}}.
\]

This field evolves according to invariants enforced by SAGES, ensuring coherence, stability, and ethical alignment.

---

### 3.7 Channels as Basis Vectors of the Lattice

The channels form the basis vectors:
\[
\mathbf{s}_1 = \text{SIC}, \quad \mathbf{s}_2 = \text{SCC}, \quad \mathbf{s}_3 = \text{ICC}.
\]

Their tensor products generate the nine cognitive cells:
\[
\mathbf{s}_i \otimes \mathbf{s}_j.
\]

These cells define the operational modes of cognition, from raw perception to identity‑modulated semantics.

---

### 3.8 Channels as Interfaces

Each channel also functions as an interface layer:
- SIC interfaces with sensors, actuators, and accessibility systems.  
- SCC interfaces with semantic engines, reasoning modules, and topological computation.  
- ICC interfaces with identity systems, provenance chains, and governance layers.  

This interface structure ensures that the cognitive lattice remains substrate‑agnostic and universally implementable.
